## Headlines

The full 10 headlines live at the top of `knowledge/issues/plpgsql.md`;
the four highest-leverage are repeated here.

### 1. The trusted-PL boundary is enforced exactly twice

In `pl_handler.c` — at `CheckFunctionValidatorAccess` (validator-only,
silent no-op on denial, **no LOG** for audit) and at the
PGC_SUSET-ness of `plpgsql.variable_conflict`. Every other "trusted
PL" property is inherited from fmgr / `pg_language` ACL machinery
upstream of the handler. **Once you've called a plpgsql function, the
trust boundary is gone.** This is the cleanest single-paragraph answer
to "what does 'trusted' actually mean for PL/pgSQL?" the corpus has
surfaced.

### 2. EXECUTE has zero injection defenses for the query body

`exec_stmt_dynexecute` (`pl_exec.c:4541`) feeds the user-computed
query string directly to `SPI_execute_extended`. **USING params are
parameterized; the query body is never auto-quoted.** EXECUTE plans
are re-parsed every call (no plancache shield). Combined with the lack
of any strongly-typed `quote_*`-returning helper in the language, this
is the canonical PL/pgSQL injection vector and it has no structural
guard. Trigger context `TG_TABLE_NAME` is catalog-derived and safe in
itself but is the most common ingredient in injection-prone
`EXECUTE 'INSERT INTO ' || TG_TABLE_NAME` patterns.

### 3. WHEN OTHERS swallows everything except QUERY_CANCELED + ASSERT_FAILURE

`pl_exec.c:1637`. By design, but creates an audit-evasion surface
when combined with subxact rollback semantics: an EXCEPTION block
hides a failed DDL/DML mid-function while still committing prior SRF
tuples already pushed to the caller's tuplestore (`pl_exec.c:3722`).
Worth a static-analyzer rule and a Phase D persona pitch.

### 4. Two never-invalidated session caches

- `cast_expr_hash` — explicitly never invalidated on `pg_cast`
  changes (`pl_exec.c:138`, file-header comment admits it).
- Simple-expr fast-path validity is entirely delegated to
  `plancache.c::CachedPlanIsSimplyValid` (`pl_exec.c:6161`).

Any missed plancache invalidation (search_path / role / function
redef) silently runs the wrong expression. These are the two trust
boundaries any "stale-plan" CVE-class issue would land on. **New
Phase D cluster: session-cache-staleness.**
