All four had been failing the **`Check`** stage (core regression) with short
run-times (taipan ~00:01:26, bushmaster ~00:03:30) — the signature of a fast
regression-diff failure, not a crash or timeout. The failing test was
`generated_virtual` (per the STATE.md trail and the fix commit's own test delta
in `src/test/regress/{sql,expected}/generated_virtual`).

**Rootcause (now understood):** the LLVM tuple-deform JIT's
`guaranteed_column_number` optimization treated a `NOT NULL` virtual generated
column as guaranteed-present-and-non-NULL, but virtual generated columns are
stored as NULL in the tuple. On these animals (`jit = on`, low `jit_above_cost`,
so JIT fires in the regression suite) the miscompiled deformer produced wrong
output — `c = 0` instead of the expected value — exactly the diff #340 reported.
`dc51167` adds `attgenerated != ATTRIBUTE_GENERATED_VIRTUAL` guards at the three
fast-path sites. See `knowledge/upstream-deltas/2026-06-19.md` deep-dive 1.

**Lesson (durable):** a regression failure that appears *only* on JIT-enabled
animals (taipan/bushmaster/canebrake/urutu are the canonical Debian-Sid JIT
quartet) and not on the mainstream gcc/clang animals points at a
JIT-vs-interpreter divergence — the interpreter (`slot_deform_heap_tuple` in
`heaptuple.c`) and the JIT (`llvmjit_deform.c`) must agree, and new column
semantics (here: VIRTUAL generated) have to be taught to *both*. When triaging a
JIT-only red, diff the two deform paths first.
