### 1. longfin / sifaka — REL_17 & REL_18 `Build`, `redefinition of typedef 'Node'` — LIKELY REAL

- **Animals:** longfin (macOS, clang) REL_18 @ 04:12:28 + REL_17 @ 04:12:02;
  sifaka REL_18 @ 04:00:19 + REL_17 @ 04:00:08.
- **Stage:** Build.
- **Error:** `error: redefinition of typedef 'Node' is a C11 feature
  [-Werror,-Wtypedef-redefinition]` (repeated).
- **Rootcause guess:** Four independent macOS/clang animals failed the *same*
  compile error on *both* stable branches REL_17 and REL_18 within a 12-minute
  window. A coordinated multi-animal, multi-branch build break is almost never a
  flake — it points to a **backpatch that introduced a duplicate `typedef …
  Node;`** without a guard, tripping clang's `-Wtypedef-redefinition` under
  `-Werror`. Master was not seen failing this, so the offending backpatch likely
  diverged from master's version of the header. Worth a glance at recent
  REL_17/18 commits touching `src/include/nodes/nodes.h` (or a header that
  forward-declares `Node`). Lesson for our own backpatch discipline: clang
  treats a redundant typedef redefinition as an error pre-C11; gcc animals
  (which dominate the farm) stay green, so a Node/typedef backpatch can look
  clean on Linux and only break the macOS animals.
