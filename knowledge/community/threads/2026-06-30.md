## 1. Deadlock detector fails to activate on a hot standby replica

- Author: Xuneng Zhou (06-29 03:14).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Deadlock+detector+fails+to+activate+on+a+hot+standby+replica>
- Reports that on a hot standby the deadlock detector can fail to fire for a
  recovery-conflict buffer-pin wait: the startup process and a pinning backend
  can wedge without the timeout ever escalating to a deadlock check. Sibling to
  the 06-28 "repeated deadlock-check signals in standby buffer pin waits"
  thread — same corner of recovery-conflict lock handling, opposite failure
  mode (no signal vs signal storm).
- The standby deadlock-timer arming around `ResolveRecoveryConflictWithBufferPin`
  is the suspect: a lost or mis-scheduled wakeup leaves the detector dormant.
- **Why it matters:** a dormant deadlock detector on a standby turns a transient
  recovery conflict into an indefinite hang with no diagnostic — the worst kind
  of availability bug because nothing logs. Pairs with the 06-28 signal-storm
  report as two halves of the same fragile timer.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-lmgr.md]], [[knowledge/idioms/deadlock-detection.md]]

## 2. 048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl hangs due to wakeup lost inside LockBufferForCleanup

- Author: Fujii Masao (06-29 01:40).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl+hangs+wakeup+lost+LockBufferForCleanup>
- A recovery TAP test (`048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl`) intermittently hangs; the
  diagnosis is a lost wakeup inside `LockBufferForCleanup()` — the cleanup-lock
  waiter isn't woken when the last pin is dropped, so it sleeps forever.
- Cleanup locks (exclusive buffer access with *zero other pins*) rely on the
  pin-drop path signalling the waiter; a missed `ProcSendSignal` / latch set is
  the classic shape of this bug.
- Touches `bufmgr.c` cleanup-lock waiting and the pin-count bookkeeping that
  decides when to wake a `BM_PIN_COUNT_WAITER`.
- **Why it matters:** a lost-wakeup in cleanup-lock acquisition is a real
  hazard for VACUUM and recovery (both take cleanup locks), not just a flaky
  test — the test is the canary for a production hang. Cleanup-lock wakeup races
  are notoriously hard to reproduce. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]],
  [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-lmgr.md]]

## 3. Missing FSM Update when Updating VM On-access

- Author: Melanie Plageman (06-29 22:32).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Missing+FSM+Update+when+Updating+VM+On-access>
- When a page's visibility-map bit is set lazily on access (the "on-access" VM
  update path, as opposed to during VACUUM), the corresponding free-space-map
  update can be skipped, leaving the FSM out of sync with the page's true free
  space after the all-visible transition.
- The fix coordinates the VM-set and FSM-adjust so an on-access all-visible
  transition keeps both maps consistent.
- Sits in the heap page-pruning / `visibilitymap_set` interaction with
  `RecordPageWithFreeSpace`.
- **Why it matters:** FSM/VM drift causes the planner and the inserter to make
  decisions on stale free-space data — wasted seq scans of all-visible pages or
  failed fast-path inserts. One of three VM/FSM-correctness reports this window,
  signalling a focused audit of the map-update paths.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/access-heap.md]], [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-freespace.md]]

  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=autovacuum+launcher+crash+assert+pgstat_count_io_op+IOOP_EXTEND>
- An assertion fires in `pgstat_count_io_op()` when the autovacuum launcher
  triggers an `IOOP_EXTEND` on the visibility map fork of `pg_database` — an I/O

  machinery's assertions consider impossible.

- **Why it matters:** a cassert-build crash in the autovacuum launcher is a
  stability bug in the one process that must never die; the IO-stats assertion
  matrix is new enough (PG16-era) that under-tested op/context combinations are
  still surfacing. [[knowledge/idioms/autovacuum-launcher.md]],
  [[knowledge/subsystems/pgstat.md]]

## 5. Why clearing the VM doesn't require registering vm buffer in wal record

- Author: Andrey Borodin (06-29 07:46).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Why+clearing+the+VM+doesn%27t+require+registering+vm+buffer+in+wal+record>
- A design-clarification thread: when a heap modification *clears* a VM bit
  (page no longer all-visible), the VM buffer is not registered in the WAL
  record, unlike when a bit is *set*. The thread asks whether this asymmetry is
  safe under crash recovery and torn-page scenarios.
- The answer hinges on the invariant that a falsely-set VM bit is a correctness
  bug (skips a needed scan) while a falsely-clear bit is merely a lost
  optimization (does an unnecessary scan) — so clears are allowed to be lost on
  replay.
- Goes to the heart of the VM WAL-logging contract in `visibilitymap.c` and the
  heap redo routines.
- **Why it matters:** the set-vs-clear WAL asymmetry is a load-bearing
  correctness invariant that's easy to "fix" wrongly; documenting *why* the
  asymmetry is safe protects future patches from over-logging or, worse,
  trusting a cleared bit. Third VM-touching thread this window.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/wal.md]], [[knowledge/subsystems/access-heap.md]]

  [[knowledge/subsystems/pgstat.md]]

## 7. Fix HAVING-to-WHERE pushdown with mismatched operator families

- Author: Tender Wang (continues from 06-29; active into 06-30).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Fix+HAVING-to-WHERE+pushdown+with+mismatched+operator+families>
- Carries forward the wrong-results planner bug first flagged in the 06-29
  window: `HAVING`→`WHERE` pushdown mis-fires when the clause's operator belongs
  to a different operator family than the grouping equality. The 06-30 messages
  refine the op-family compatibility predicate.
- The pushdown only preserves semantics when the operator's family matches the
  grouping key's equality family; the patch tightens the precondition.
- Lives in `subquery_planner` / the `hasHavingQual` lowering in
  `grouping_planner`.
- **Why it matters:** still a wrong-results bug (the most serious class), and
  the multi-day discussion shows how subtle the equality-vs-ordering op-family
  distinction is — exactly the kind of fix worth getting right before the CF
  transition rather than rushing. [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]]

## 8. Do not scan index in right table if left-join condition is false from left columns

- Author: Andrei Lepikhov (06-29 08:20).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Do+not+scan+index+in+right+table+if+condition+for+left+join+evaluates+to+false>
- Proposes a planner/executor optimization for left joins: if the join
  condition can be evaluated to *false* using only left-side columns, the
  inner (right) index scan for that outer row can be skipped entirely — it can
  produce no match.
- Effectively a runtime short-circuit on the inner side of a nestloop when the
  outer-only predicate already disqualifies the row.
- Touches nestloop-join execution and the planner's decision about which
  predicates are outer-only evaluable.
- **Why it matters:** skipping provably-empty inner scans is a real win for
  selective left joins with expensive inner index access; the risk is getting
  the "outer-only and definitely-false" analysis exactly right so no valid
  match is dropped. [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]],
  [[knowledge/subsystems/executor.md]]

## 9. Adding basic NUMA awareness

- Author: Jakub Wartak (06-29 07:42).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Adding+basic+NUMA+awareness>
- A long-running infrastructure thread, active again this window, on giving
  PostgreSQL basic NUMA awareness — interleaving or node-pinning large shared
  memory regions (shared buffers, the proc array) so multi-socket machines don't
  pay remote-memory penalties on hot structures.
- Discussion covers `libnuma` integration, which shmem regions benefit most, and
  how to keep the feature optional / portable.
- Foundational shared-memory-allocation work touching the shmem setup paths.
- **Why it matters:** on large multi-socket servers, NUMA-blind shared-buffer
  placement is a measurable throughput tax; even "basic" awareness is a step
  toward closing the gap with hand-tuned deployments. A strategic infra item
  that recurs across cycles. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]],
  [[knowledge/idioms/shared-memory-startup.md]]

## 11. [SP-]GiST index-only-scan visibility bug

- Author: solai v (06-29 04:08).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=SP-GiST+IOS+visibility+bug>
- Reports a visibility-check bug in SP-GiST (and possibly GiST) index-only
  scans: a tuple can be returned from the index without a correct
  all-visible / heap-visibility check, risking returning data from a
  not-actually-visible tuple.
- The IOS fast path trusts the VM bit to skip the heap fetch; if the AM's IOS
  visibility wiring is wrong, an IOS can leak rows it shouldn't.
- Sits in the SP-GiST `amgettuple` / index-only-scan visibility recheck path.
- **Why it matters:** an IOS returning rows that fail visibility is a
  correctness bug that can surface as phantom or stale rows under concurrency —
  the precise failure mode index-only scans are supposed to guard against. Index
  AM visibility wiring is exactly the kind of thing that rots silently.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/access-spgist.md]], [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-visibilitymap.md]]

## 12. Collation & ctype method table, and extension hooks

- Author: Noah Misch (06-29 01:29).
  Archive: <https://www.postgresql.org/search/?m=1&q=Collation+%26+ctype+method+table+and+extension+hooks>
- Proposes refactoring the collation / ctype provider interface into a method
  table and exposing extension hooks, so a new collation provider (beyond libc /
  ICU / builtin) can be supplied by an extension instead of being hard-wired in
  core.

- Foundational refactor of the locale layer with an extensibility payoff.
- **Why it matters:** collation provider churn (the ICU-version-instability

  collation behavior from the libc/ICU release treadmill.
