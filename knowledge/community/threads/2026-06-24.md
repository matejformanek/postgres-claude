## 1. 048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl hangs due to wakeup lost inside LockBufferForCleanup

- Reporter: Xuneng Zhou. Active 2026-06-23 02:15 → 16:20.
- A TAP recovery test (`048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl`) hangs because a wakeup is
  lost while a backend waits in `LockBufferForCleanup()` — the cleanup-lock
  acquirer is parked on its buffer-pin condition variable and a concurrent
  pin-drop fails to signal it.
- The race is in the buffer-pin / cleanup-lock handshake: `LockBufferForCleanup`
  sleeps until the other pinners release, but the wakeup path
  (`UnpinBuffer` → `ConditionVariableBroadcast`) can be missed under a specific
  interleaving, leaving the vacuum backend stuck indefinitely.
- Why it matters: cleanup-lock acquisition is the gate for heap-page vacuuming
  and the freeze horizon; a lost wakeup there is a hang, not just a slowdown,
  and it surfaces as a flaky recovery test that masks a real shmem-signalling
  bug. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]]

## 2. Handle concurrent drop when doing whole database vacuum

- Authors: cca5507 (06-23 05:20); Bharath Rupireddy, Michael Paquier.
- `VACUUM` (no table list) enumerates every relation, then visits each — but a
  relation can be `DROP`ped between enumeration and visit, so the per-table
  open can error out and abort the whole-database pass.
- Patch makes the per-relation open in the whole-DB vacuum loop tolerant of a
  missing relation (skip-and-continue) rather than letting a concurrent drop
  fail the entire command. Paquier and Rupireddy debating the exact
  skip/`try_relation_open` shape.
- Why it matters: autovacuum and manual database-wide VACUUM run for a long
  time against a live catalog; a concurrent DDL drop should not be able to kill
  the pass. [[knowledge/subsystems/access-heap.md]]

## 3. Why clearing the VM doesn't require registering vm buffer in wal record

- Author: Melanie Plageman (06-23 23:55, 06-24 00:01).
- Question/explainer thread on the visibility-map WAL protocol: clearing a VM
  bit (page no longer all-visible) does not need the VM buffer registered in
  the heap-modify WAL record, because replay re-derives and clears the bit from
  the heap change itself; only *setting* the bit needs the explicit VM record.
- Clarifies the asymmetry in `heap_xlog_*` replay: clear-on-modify is implicit,
  set-all-visible is explicit (`XLOG_HEAP2_VISIBLE`).
- Why it matters: the VM/WAL asymmetry is a recurring source of confusion and
  of corruption bugs when a new heap path forgets to clear the bit; documenting
  the rule reduces that class. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]]

## 4. Problems with get_actual_variable_range's VISITED_PAGES_LIMIT

- Authors: Peter Geoghegan (06-23 20:06); Jakub Wartak (06-23 08:48).
- `get_actual_variable_range()` (used by the planner to find the real min/max of
  an indexed column for selectivity) caps how many index pages it will visit via
  `VISITED_PAGES_LIMIT`. The thread shows pathological cases where many dead/
  killed tuples make the scan blow past the limit or give bad range estimates.
- Discussion of whether the limit should scale, interact with kill-prior-tuple
  index hints, or be replaced by a smarter early-out.
- Why it matters: this function runs at plan time on every range/inequality
  against an indexed column; a slow or wrong answer here degrades planning
  latency and row estimates fleet-wide. [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]]

## 7. Fix HAVING-to-WHERE pushdown with mismatched operator families

- Authors: Richard Guo (06-23 01:20, 02:52); Zsolt Parragi (15:52).
- The planner pushes a `HAVING` clause down to `WHERE` when it references no
  aggregates, but the transform can be wrong when the clause's operator belongs
  to a different operator family than the one the equivalence/index machinery
  assumes — producing a clause that filters differently after pushdown.
- Fix tightens the eligibility check so only opfamily-consistent predicates are
  pushed.
- Why it matters: HAVING→WHERE pushdown is a correctness-sensitive rewrite;
  an opfamily mismatch silently changes results, not just plans.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]]
