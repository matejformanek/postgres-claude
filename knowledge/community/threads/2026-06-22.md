## 1. Global temporary tables — Dean Rasheed

URL: `/message-id/CAEZATCUbTj5MraZsimV8VYd1M3zrhP8soVJRZ%2BWvYyea6moefg%40mail.gmail.com`
(Andrew Dunstan replies same window.)

- New thread reviving the long-standing global-temporary-tables feature: a
  table whose *definition* is shared cluster-wide but whose *data* is
  session-private, contrasted with today's session-local `TEMP` tables that
  must be re-created each session.
- Touches catalog visibility (one `pg_class` row, per-session storage),
  relcache, and the per-session `relfilenode`/storage allocation model.
- Dunstan engaging on the design tradeoffs — a recurring hard problem because
  GTT cuts across catalog, storage, stats, and autovacuum.

Why it matters: GTT is one of the most-requested missing features; the
sticking point has always been per-session storage + statistics without
bloating shared catalogs. [[knowledge/subsystems/utils-cache.md]]

## 3. TID recycling race during nbtree index-only scans that run on a standby — Matthias van de Meent

URL: `/message-id/CAEze2WighSz7jbnw4L0KgQEBg_4%2BHJhDKdYwQTgeyDTXHwFvfQ%40mail.gmail.com`

- Reports a race where an index-only scan on a hot standby can observe a TID
  that has been recycled (heap line pointer reused) because the standby's
  view of the kill/recycle horizon differs from the primary's.
- Intersects the nbtree microvacuum / `kill_prior_tuple` machinery and the
  visibility-map check that lets IOS skip the heap fetch.

Why it matters: a recycled-TID race on a standby risks an index-only scan
returning a wrong row — a correctness bug, not a perf one.
[[knowledge/idioms/index-only-scan-vm-check.md]]

## 4. 048_vacuum_horizon_floor.pl hangs due to wakeup lost inside LockBufferForCleanup — Xuneng Zhou

URL: `/message-id/CABPTF7W6PsSCOby4JDo9UZunsbHu91FHB0u1K0WUvzWjQ1WRJg%40mail.gmail.com`

- A recovery TAP test hangs because a buffer-pin wakeup is lost inside
  `LockBufferForCleanup` — the cleanup-lock waiter is not signalled when the
  last conflicting pin drops, so vacuum's super-exclusive acquisition stalls.
- Investigation of the pin-count / `BM_PIN_COUNT_WAITER` handshake and the
  ProcWaitForSignal wakeup path.

Why it matters: a lost cleanup-lock wakeup is a classic shared-buffer
signalling bug; it manifests as a vacuum hang, hard to diagnose.
[[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]]

## 6. Fix handling of copy_file_range() return value — Peter Eisentraut

URL: `/message-id/3208cf7a-c7f3-41eb-92f6-33cbeff4df40%40eisentraut.org`
(Nazir Bilal Yavuz, Kyotaro Horiguchi, Yingying Chen reviewing.)

- Reports that callers of `copy_file_range()` mishandle short copies: the
  syscall may copy fewer bytes than requested and must be called in a loop,
  but some PG call sites assume a single full copy.
- Touches the file-clone fast paths used by `pg_upgrade`/`CREATE DATABASE`

Why it matters: a mishandled short `copy_file_range` silently truncates a
copied relation file — a data-loss-shaped bug on the FS-clone path.
