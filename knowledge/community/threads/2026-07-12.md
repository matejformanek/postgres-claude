## 1. Reduce cleanup lock contention on standby replay
- Author: Satyanarayana Narlapuram · 2026-07-11 02:47
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAHg%2BQDdVtRyYaYb8Un9uE4AXWfoYQ6ahGe6n%2BAje2SFgADEAjA%40mail.gmail.com
- Bullets:
  - Standby WAL replay takes a **cleanup lock** (super-exclusive buffer pin)
    on pages it prunes, and on busy read replicas that serializes replay
    against every reader still pinning the page — the recovery process stalls
    waiting for pins to drain.
  - Proposal reworks the replay path so pruning/freezing records that don't
    physically move tuples can proceed under a weaker lock, deferring the
    cleanup-lock requirement to records that actually need it.
  - Thread continues the 3-patch series first posted this week; review is
    circling the correctness boundary — which redo routines are safe to
    downgrade without breaking index-scan visibility on the standby.
- Why it matters: cleanup-lock stalls are a top cause of replica replay lag
  under read load; a correct downgrade would materially cut standby lag.
- Corpus: [[buffer-manager]] (cleanup lock / super-exclusive pin),
  [[locking]] (buffer pin/content lock rank), [[physical-replication]] (redo).

## 2. relfilenode statistics
- Authors: Michael Paquier, Bertrand Drouvot · 2026-07-11 03:44 → 12:08
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/alG8F9lVldB3WseN%40paquier.xyz
- Bullets:
  - Long-running series to attach cumulative statistics to **relfilenode**
    rather than relation OID, so I/O counters survive a rewrite (VACUUM FULL,
    CLUSTER, TRUNCATE) that swaps the underlying storage.
  - Today's four-message burst worked the pgstat kind registration and the
    drop/create ordering when a relfilenode is recycled — the hazard is a
    stale stats entry outliving its storage and colliding with a reused number.
  - Bertrand and Michael converged on keying the entry so a rewrite migrates
    (not orphans) the counters; open question is the flush path at drop.
- Why it matters: today relation I/O stats reset on every rewrite, blinding
  DBAs to churn on frequently-repacked tables; this closes that gap.
- Corpus: [[pgstat-framework]] (variable-numbered stats kinds, drop ordering).

## 3. sequencesync worker race with REFRESH SEQUENCES
- Authors: Amit Kapila, vignesh C, Noah Misch · 2026-07-11 05:01 → 20:14
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAA4eK1KFgVFdocPt4TPXmkkOfwY7YNRfG-0jRVKCuOaMNWSCUA%40mail.gmail.com
- Bullets:
  - The new logical-replication **sequence sync** worker races with a
    concurrent `ALTER SUBSCRIPTION ... REFRESH PUBLICATION SEQUENCES`: the
    refresh can rewrite the pg_subscription_rel set the worker is mid-copy on.
  - Noah Misch's 07-11 reply extends an earlier defect sweep — enumerating the
    specific interleavings where a sequence ends up half-synced or the worker
    loops on a since-removed entry.
  - Direction is to gate the refresh behind the worker's slot state (mirroring
    how tablesync coordinates with REFRESH PUBLICATION for tables).
- Why it matters: sequence replication is a headline PG20 logical-rep feature;
  a sync-vs-refresh race would surface as silently wrong sequence values on the
  subscriber.
- Corpus: [[logical-replication]] (tablesync/sequencesync coordination).

## 6. [PATCH] Batched clock sweep to reduce cross-socket atomic contention
- Author: Greg Burd · 2026-07-11 15:54
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/9cfdf3f2-b799-485a-8b5a-5159c00ff2df%40app.fastmail.com
- Bullets:
  - Replaces the single global `nextVictimBuffer` atomic advanced one slot at a
    time with a **batched** claim: a backend grabs a run of candidate victims in
    one atomic step, cutting the cross-socket cache-line ping-pong on the sweep
    counter under high buffer-eviction pressure.
  - New revision responds to earlier NUMA-scaling review; benchmarks target
    many-core eviction-bound workloads.
  - Contention target is the same StrategyControl hot counter flagged in prior
    weeks' shared-buffer-hash restructuring threads.
- Why it matters: the clock-sweep victim counter is a known scalability
  bottleneck on large sockets; batching is a low-risk, high-leverage fix.
- Corpus: [[buffer-manager]] (clock sweep / StrategyGetBuffer), [[locking]]
  (atomics, cross-socket contention).

## 7. allow spread checkpoints when changing checksums online
- Authors: Tomas Vondra, Fujii Masao · 2026-07-11 17:05 → 20:17
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/8154744d-f5f9-43a6-8941-c80ad31bb0e2%40vondra.me
- Bullets:
  - Online checksum enable/disable currently forces an **immediate** checkpoint
    at each state transition; the patch lets those checkpoints be spread
    (throttled) like normal ones to avoid an I/O spike on large clusters.
  - Fujii Masao's review probes the interaction with the checksum-worker state
    machine — a spread checkpoint must not let a page slip between "in progress"
    and "on" without being checksummed.
  - Ties into the same `datachecksum_state.c` cluster as the SIGINT-handling
    thread below.
- Why it matters: online checksum conversion on a big DB is I/O-heavy; spreading
  the checkpoints makes it operable on a live primary.
- Corpus: [[wal-and-xlog]] (checkpoint spreading / CheckPointGuts).

## 8. index prefetching
- Author: Peter Geoghegan · 2026-07-11 17:08
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAH2-Wz%3DDhG5%3Dsw9P1A5%3DbnXxCz4UJaWgH5WQA-3pEckXRucZdQ%40mail.gmail.com
- Bullets:
  - The index-scan-over-read-stream series continues: driving heap fetches for
    index scans through the **read_stream** API so heap pages are prefetched
    ahead of the index cursor rather than faulted one-at-a-time.
  - Revision works the interaction with mark/restore and the kill-prior-tuple

  prefetch-aware; this is where AIO's latency win reaches OLTP index lookups.
- Corpus: [[aio-readstream]] (read_stream consumers), [[executor-and-planner]].

## 11. pg_xmin_horizon: a system view of everything pinning the xmin horizon

  `gap:ddl-deparse-ruleutils` harvester tag (flagged ≥3 in #586).
