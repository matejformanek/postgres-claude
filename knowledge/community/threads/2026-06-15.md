## 1. index prefetching
**Author:** Peter Geoghegan · 2026-06-14 18:54
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAH2-Wzn%2BC%3DmAMv9aW3Skfh80JPpHKT3yM%3DDYwkrrhYyG2f%2B_vg%40mail.gmail.com)

- New revision in the long-running effort to teach index scans to issue
  read-ahead on heap pages they're about to visit, hiding I/O latency behind
  the index traversal rather than serializing fetch-then-read.
- Sits on top of the streaming-read / `read_stream` infrastructure; the
  contention point is how much look-ahead the AM layer can promise without
  breaking the `amgettuple` ordering contract or holding buffer pins too long.
- Geoghegan's framing keeps coming back to the boundary between the index AM
  (which knows the *next TIDs*) and the table AM (which owns the *heap fetch*).

**Why it matters:** index prefetching is one of the headline PG20 performance
items; it reshapes the `amgettuple` → `heap_fetch` handshake that every
index-scan plan node depends on. [[knowledge/architecture/access-methods.md]]
· [[knowledge/data-structures/indexamroutine.md]] · [[knowledge/subsystems/executor.md]]

## 2. Reduce LEFT/FULL JOIN to ANTI JOIN in more cases
**Author:** Richard Guo · 2026-06-15 09:08
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAMbWs4_zJhF0fyng6SMYVnBvxPK1hhTKcn%3DFN%3DKdnnoa4_DiuA%40mail.gmail.com)

- Planner transform: when a LEFT/FULL join's result is filtered by an
  `IS NULL` test on the nullable side (the classic "anti-join in disguise"),
  recognize more of those shapes and rewrite to a real ANTI JOIN.
- ANTI JOIN lets the executor stop at the first match per outer row and opens
  hash/merge anti-join paths the LEFT-join form wouldn't cost as cheaply.
- The widening is about catching cases the current `reduce_outer_joins` /
  qual-analysis misses, without misfiring on quals that aren't strict.

**Why it matters:** a pure planner-side win — same answer, cheaper path — that
touches outer-join qual reasoning, an area where strictness mistakes silently
change results. [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]] · [[knowledge/idioms/cost-join-paths.md]]

## 3. injection_points: switch wait/wakeup to use atomics rather than latches
**Author:** Andrey Borodin → Michael Paquier · 2026-06-14 09:23 (latest 06-15 00:14)
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/EAEE7784-1CE6-43CD-9008-2F726F3761C0%40yandex-team.ru)

- Reworks the test-only `injection_points` wait/wakeup primitive to coordinate
  via shared atomics instead of per-backend latches.
- Motivation: latch-based wakeup is racy/heavier for the precise "park here
  until the test wakes you" semantics injection points want in isolation tests.
- Paquier (the injection-points maintainer) engaged the same day — small,
  self-contained, but touches the test scaffolding a lot of TAP/iso suites lean on.

**Why it matters:** injection points are how modern PG races get
deterministically reproduced in tests; making their wait primitive atomic-based
is infrastructure the whole hacker community's regression coverage rides on.
[[knowledge/data-structures/latch-waiteventset.md]] · [[knowledge/idioms/lwlock-rank-discipline.md]]

## 4. Separate catalog_xmin from xmin in walsender hot standby feedback
**Author:** Rui Zhao · 2026-06-15 02:48
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/57E2263F-455D-49A5-A16D-E66562DBC027%40gmail.com)

- Proposes the walsender report `catalog_xmin` independently of the data
  `xmin` in hot-standby feedback, so the primary can hold back catalog tuples
  for logical slots on the standby without over-retaining heap tuples.
- The two horizons have different retention needs; collapsing them inflates
  bloat on the primary when only catalog visibility needs protecting.
- Ties into the cascaded-logical-replication / failover-slot story where a
  standby owns logical slots.

**Why it matters:** `catalog_xmin` vs `xmin` is a load-bearing MVCC-horizon
distinction; getting feedback to carry both is what lets logical slots survive
on standbys without bloating the primary. [[knowledge/architecture/replication.md]]
· [[knowledge/architecture/mvcc.md]] · [[knowledge/data-structures/snapshot-lifecycle.md]]

## 5. Return value of XLogInsertRecord() for XLOG_SWITCH record
**Author:** ZizhuanLiu X-MAN · 2026-06-14 14:37 (active thread through 06-15)
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/tencent_C26B62ED5BC2129C9AA2F10B9EA30D8DD605%40qq.com)

- Questions what LSN `XLogInsertRecord()` should hand back for an
  `XLOG_SWITCH` record, which intentionally pads to the next segment boundary.
- The returned end-LSN can land *past* the switch point; callers that treat it
  as "bytes written here" can miscompute flush/wait targets.
- Several round-trips on 06-15 nailing down the exact contract and whether any
  caller is actually mislead today.

**Why it matters:** `XLOG_SWITCH` is the seam between WAL records and segment
geometry; a wrong end-LSN feeds into archiving and replication wait logic.
[[knowledge/architecture/wal.md]] · [[knowledge/subsystems/access-transam.md]]

## 6. Allow wal_log_hints to be changed without restart
**Author:** Bertrand Drouvot · 2026-06-15 13:40
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/ajAAwSFy0WVMroyk%40bdtpg)

- Proposes demoting `wal_log_hints` from `PGC_POSTMASTER` so it can be toggled
  without a full restart (today changing it requires bouncing the postmaster).
- The hard part is that `wal_log_hints` interacts with checksums and the
  full-page-image decision at hint-bit set time; flipping it mid-flight must
  not leave a window where hint updates skip WAL but a torn page assumes they did.
- Likely needs the change to take effect only at a safe boundary (next
  checkpoint) rather than instantly.

**Why it matters:** moving a WAL-correctness GUC off `PGC_POSTMASTER` is exactly
the kind of change where the GucContext choice *is* the correctness argument.
[[knowledge/idioms/hint-bits-setbufferdirty.md]] · [[knowledge/idioms/guc-variables.md]]

## 8. LLVM JIT: any JIT-compiled query crashes (SIGILL) on libLLVM 19 + ASAN
**Author:** Matheus Alcantara · 2026-06-15 09:46
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/DJ9IZJYU9J00.345IGM5JLMMNC%40gmail.com)

- Bug report: with libLLVM 19 under an ASAN build, every JIT-compiled query
  dies with SIGILL (illegal instruction) — i.e. the emitted code is bad or the
  ORC/relocation step mismatches the runtime.
- Smells like an LLVM-19 API/relocation-model change PG's JIT provider hasn't
  adapted to, surfaced only because ASAN perturbs the codegen/memory layout.
- Reproducer is "any JIT query", so it's a clean bisect target against the

**Why it matters:** PG's JIT tracks a fast-moving LLVM API; SIGILL on a current
LLVM is a portability regression that blocks JIT on new toolchains.

## 9. Fix ALTER DOMAIN VALIDATE CONSTRAINT locking
**Author:** Fujii Masao · 2026-06-14 15:52 (reviewed by Chao Li 06-15)
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAHGQGwFAVGOvJxA1HdRN-DoVK-Fn1W4S%2BO1O%2BGpNO-W2mdjiYA%40mail.gmail.com)

- `ALTER DOMAIN ... VALIDATE CONSTRAINT` takes the wrong lock strength on the
  domain (or its dependent objects), opening a concurrency hole or, conversely,
  over-locking.
- The fix aligns the lock level with what validation actually reads/writes in
  the catalog and the tables using the domain.
- Reviewed same-day; the interesting part is matching DDL lock level to the
  real hazard, the recurring "is `ShareUpdateExclusive` enough here?" question.

**Why it matters:** DDL lock-level bugs are silent until two sessions race;
domain validation touches every table column of that type, widening the blast
radius. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-lmgr.md]] · [[knowledge/idioms/locking-overview.md]]

## 10. Handle concurrent drop when doing whole database vacuum
**Author:** cca5507 → Kyotaro Horiguchi · 2026-06-14 07:12 (latest 06-15 05:54)
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/tencent_F9D483523BB0D082C2EFDA80142F192DBC07%40qq.com)

- Database-wide `VACUUM` builds a relation list, then vacuums each; if a
  relation is dropped concurrently between list-build and processing, the loop
  must skip rather than error.
- Patch hardens that window so a `DROP TABLE` racing an in-progress
  `VACUUM` (no table named) doesn't abort the whole vacuum.
- Horiguchi engaged on the right place to re-check existence (open-with-missing-
  ok vs catalog snapshot) so the skip is race-free.

**Why it matters:** whole-DB vacuum is what autovacuum and nightly maintenance
lean on; an abort on a concurrent drop turns a routine job into a failed one.
[[knowledge/subsystems/access-heap.md]] · [[knowledge/idioms/autovacuum-launcher.md]]

## 11. REPACK CONCURRENTLY fails on tables with generated columns
**Author:** Srinath Reddy Sadipiralla · 2026-06-14 03:56
[archive](https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAFC%2Bb6pbdKLp_NP2MZaRFxmKmZK88janhpsYmkW4wY%2BXpwOOrw%40mail.gmail.com)

- Bug in the new `REPACK` command (the CLUSTER/VACUUM FULL successor): the
  CONCURRENTLY variant chokes on tables with generated columns.
- Likely the concurrent rebuild path re-evaluates or mis-copies generated

**Why it matters:** REPACK CONCURRENTLY is meant to be the online table-rewrite
