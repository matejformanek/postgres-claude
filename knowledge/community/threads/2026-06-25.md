## 1. Out-of-Cycle release? (BUG #19490: streaming standby stops applying WAL on MultiXactOffset SLRU)

- Authors: Michael Banck (06-25 09:30); follow-on from the BUG #19490 report.
- A streaming standby can wedge — it stops applying WAL — when replay touches
  the MultiXactOffset SLRU under a specific offset/page boundary condition. The
  thread debates whether the severity (silent replay stall on a replica)
  warrants an out-of-cycle point release rather than waiting for the scheduled
  minor.
- The stall is in SLRU page access during multixact replay on the standby: the
  startup process blocks/loops instead of advancing, so the replica silently
  falls behind with no error surfaced to the operator.
- Why it matters: a standby that *stops applying WAL without erroring* is the
  worst failure shape for HA — failover targets drift stale while monitoring
  still shows the walreceiver connected. MultiXact SLRU replay bugs are a
  recurring class. [[knowledge/idioms/multixact-slru.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/slru-page-replacement.md]]

## 2. [WIP] Pipelined Recovery

- Author: Xuneng Zhou (06-25 07:47).
- WIP proposal to pipeline WAL recovery: overlap WAL record decode/read-ahead
  with apply so the startup process isn't serially stalling on each record's
  buffer reads. Aims to close the long-standing gap where a primary with many
  parallel-writing backends out-runs a single-threaded replay on the standby.
- Leans on the read-stream / prefetch infrastructure (`recovery_prefetch`) but
  pushes further toward decode/apply pipelining rather than just block
  prefetch.
- Why it matters: single-threaded redo is the structural ceiling on replica
  catch-up and failover RTO; any credible pipelining of replay is a headline
  scalability change. [[knowledge/idioms/xlog-region-replay.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/read-stream-prefetch.md]]

## 3. autovacuum launcher crash: assert in pgstat_count_io_op (IOOP_EXTEND on pg_database's VM)

- Author: Melanie Plageman (06-24 18:59).
- An assertion fires in `pgstat_count_io_op()` when an `IOOP_EXTEND` is counted
  against the visibility map of a shared catalog (`pg_database`) — a relation/
  fork/io-op combination the pgstat IO matrix didn't expect, crashing the
  autovacuum launcher under cassert.
- Surfaces a gap in the pgstat IO-operation validity table: which (object,
  context, op) tuples are legal for a shared-relation VM fork extend.
- Why it matters: the pgstat IO accounting matrix is consulted on every buffer
  op; an over-strict assert there turns a benign shared-catalog VM extend into
  a launcher crash. Same VM/pgstat surface as Plageman's 06-24 VM-WAL explainer.
  [[knowledge/idioms/pgstat-flush-timing.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/visibility-map-update.md]]

## 4. [PATCH] Don't call ereport(ERROR) from recovery target GUC assign hooks

- Author: Michael Paquier (06-25 08:00).
- The `recovery_target*` GUC assign hooks can `ereport(ERROR)`, but assign hooks
  run at a point in GUC application where throwing is unsafe / can leave GUC
  state half-applied. Patch moves the validation into the check hook (where
  rejecting is safe) and keeps the assign hook non-throwing.
- Concrete instance of the GUC check-vs-assign contract: validation belongs in
  the check hook; the assign hook must be side-effect-only and must not longjmp.
- Why it matters: a classic, easy-to-reintroduce GUC footgun — an `ERROR` from
  an assign hook can break SIGHUP reload or recovery startup in ways that are
  hard to reproduce. [[knowledge/idioms/guc-variables.md]]

## 5. Deadlock detector fails to activate on a hot standby replica / repeated deadlock-check signals in standby buffer-pin waits

- Authors: Vitaly Davydov (06-24 15:16); Xuneng Zhou ([PATCH] 06-25 11:58).
- Two adjacent standby-locking threads. (a) On a hot-standby replica the
  deadlock detector can fail to activate for a backend blocked behind the
  startup process's buffer-pin conflict, so a genuine wait never gets a
  deadlock check. (b) Conversely, a backend parked in a standby buffer-pin wait
  can be re-signalled for deadlock checks repeatedly, wasting work.
- Both center on the recovery-conflict / buffer-pin wait path
  (`ResolveRecoveryConflictWithBufferPin`) and how its timeout arms the
  deadlock-check signal versus the normal lock-wait path.
- Why it matters: recovery-conflict handling on hot standby is subtle and
  under-tested; a deadlock detector that mis-fires (or never fires) on a
  replica is both a hang risk and a CPU-burn risk.
  [[knowledge/idioms/deadlock-detection.md]] /
  [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]]

## 6. Interrupts vs signals

- Author: Heikki Linnakangas (06-24 20:25).
- Design thread on reworking the backend's interrupt model — separating the
  abstract "interrupt" (CHECK_FOR_INTERRUPTS pending work) from the OS signal
  delivery mechanism, so more interrupt sources can be added without piggybacking
  on the limited POSIX signal set and the `SIGUSR1`/multiplexed-reason scheme.
- Touches the latch / WaitEventSet machinery and the `ProcSignal` multiplexing
  that currently overloads a handful of signals with many logical reasons.
- Why it matters: the signal-multiplexing scheme is load-bearing and brittle;
  cleanly decoupling interrupts from signals would unblock a class of features
  (and remove a class of races) across the backend.
  [[knowledge/data-structures/latch-waiteventset.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/sinvaladt-broadcast.md]]

## 7. Fix unsafe coding in ResourceOwnerReleaseAll()

- Author: Tom Lane (06-25 05:15).
- `ResourceOwnerReleaseAll()` iterates and releases resources in a way that is
  unsafe if release callbacks mutate the owner's array mid-iteration; the patch
  tightens the loop so concurrent removal during release can't skip or
  double-visit an entry.
- Continues the recent ResourceOwner hardening line (cf. the #19527
  resowner/pgcrypto double-free pair watched 06-24).
- Why it matters: ResourceOwner is the universal cleanup spine — every
  transaction/portal/subxact abort walks it; an iteration bug there is a
  use-after-free or leak across the whole backend.
  [[knowledge/data-structures/resourceowner.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/abort-transaction-cleanup.md]]

## 8. Adjusting hash join memory limit to handle batch explosion

- Author: Ben Mejia (06-24 23:37).
- When a hash join badly mis-estimates and the build side doesn't fit, the
  number of batches can explode; each batch carries fixed per-batch overhead, so
  the total memory blows past `work_mem` even though each batch is "within
  budget". Proposal adjusts how the batch count interacts with the memory limit
  to bound the aggregate overhead.
- The pathology: doubling batches to respect per-batch `work_mem` increases the
  *number* of buffered batch files, and that metadata is itself unaccounted.
- Why it matters: hash-join batch explosion is a real OOM vector under skew /
  bad stats; bounding the meta-overhead makes `work_mem` mean what operators
  think it means. [[knowledge/idioms/parallel-hash-join.md]] /
  [[knowledge/subsystems/executor.md]]

## 9. Adding basic NUMA awareness

- Author: Tomas Vondra (06-24 20:26).
- Proposal to make a few hot shared-memory regions NUMA-aware — interleaving or
  node-pinning shared buffers / PGPROC-class arrays so a backend isn't paying
  remote-node latency on every access to a structure that happens to live on
  one socket's memory.
- Early/basic scope: get the allocation + first-touch policy right for the
  biggest shmem consumers before attempting per-backend affinity.
- Why it matters: on large multi-socket boxes, NUMA placement of `shared_buffers`
  and the proc array is a measurable throughput factor; PG currently leaves it
  to the OS default. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md]] /
  [[knowledge/data-structures/pgproc-fields.md]]

## 10. Proposal: Conflict log history table for Logical Replication

- Author: Dilip Kumar (06-24 07:22).
- Proposes a persistent history table recording apply-side conflicts (the
  insert_exists / update_differ / delete_missing classes surfaced by the
  conflict-detection work) so operators can audit and reconcile divergence
  after the fact, rather than only seeing conflicts in the log.
- Builds directly on the apply-worker conflict-resolution infrastructure; the
  open questions are retention, schema, and who writes the rows.
- Why it matters: conflict *detection* landed recently, but without a durable
  record operators can't reconcile; a history table is the natural next step for
  active-active / bidirectional logical replication.
  [[knowledge/idioms/apply-conflict-resolution.md]]

## 11. Improve row estimation with multi-column unique indexes

- Author: Anthonin Bonnefoy (06-24 08:24).
- The planner under-uses multi-column unique constraints when estimating
  selectivity: an equality on all columns of a unique index guarantees at most
  one row, but the estimator may still multiply per-column selectivities and
  over/under-count. Patch teaches selectivity estimation to recognize the
  full-unique-match case.
- Distinct from extended statistics — this is a direct uniqueness proof from the
  index definition.
- Why it matters: row-count estimates drive join order and method selection;
  recognizing a guaranteed-unique match removes a common source of bad plans on
  composite keys. [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/analyze-mcv-histogram-correlation.md]]

## 12. [PATCH] Fix sequence value may rollback after CREATE DATABASE TEMPLATE with WAL_LOG strategy

- Author: Bharath Rupireddy (06-24 20:06).
- With the `WAL_LOG` createdb strategy, a sequence copied into the new database
  from the template can have its on-disk value effectively roll back, because
  the sequence's WAL/relfilenode state isn't flushed/ordered correctly relative
  to the database-copy WAL. Patch corrects the ordering so the copied sequence's
  last-logged value survives.
- Specific to the newer `STRATEGY = WAL_LOG` path (vs the legacy file-copy
  strategy), which logs the copy instead of checkpointing.
- Why it matters: a silently-rolled-back sequence after `CREATE DATABASE`
  yields duplicate keys downstream — a correctness bug masked as a rare race.
  [[knowledge/idioms/relfilenumber-rewrite.md]] /
  [[knowledge/idioms/wal-record-construction.md]]
