### 3. Why clearing the VM doesn't require registering the vm buffer in the WAL record
- URL: `/message-id/CAHGQGwF_PzOv5y7ucFh7Fqqqa8ar83zYwvWugqarRD6%2B7GCtEQ%40mail.gmail.com`
- Source: pgsql-hackers — **[continuation of 07-19 vm-buffer thread]**
- Design-rationale question: why the heap-update path can *clear* a
  visibility-map bit without registering the VM page as a separate
  WAL-logged buffer, when *setting* the bit does register it. The answer
  turns on redo re-deriving the clear from the heap change.
- Skill tag: `wal-and-xlog` (+ `vacuum-autovacuum` for the VM semantics)
- Ideal answer outline: `heap_update`/`heap_insert` clear the VM bit as a
  side effect that redo reconstructs from the heap WAL record itself, so
  no separate registration is needed; setting `PD_ALL_VISIBLE` +
  `visibilitymap_set` *does* WAL-log because there's no other record to
  derive it from; `wal-and-xlog` skill (buffer registration) — VM
  torn-write safety argument.
