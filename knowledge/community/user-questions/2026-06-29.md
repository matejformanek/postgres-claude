## 1. [PATCH] Don't call ereport(ERROR) from recovery target GUC assign hooks
- URL: https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/ajzgIRrl8378bnlt%40paquier.xyz
- Source: pgsql-hackers
- Summary: A recovery-target GUC's `assign_hook` raises `ereport(ERROR)`, which
  is unsafe because assign hooks run at a point where a longjmp can leave the
  GUC machinery in an inconsistent state. The patch moves the validation into
  the `check_hook` (where soft-failure via `GUC_check_errmsg` is legal) and
  keeps the assign hook side-effect-free.
- Skill tag: `gucs-config` (+ `error-handling`)
- Ideal answer: cite `knowledge/idioms/` GUC check/assign/show split — check
  hook validates and may reject, assign hook must be non-failing; cross-ref the
  `gucs-config` skill's "string-GUC guc_malloc rules" and the
  `error-handling` skill on why ereport in a longjmp-restricted context is a
  hazard.
