## 1. BM_IO_ERROR flag is lost in TerminateBufferIO due to order of operations in UnlockBufHdrExt
- **List:** pgsql-hackers
- **Message-id:** https://www.postgresql.org/message-id/CAAKRu_YX9KsmZUyJQj%2BcQa9gs_aCwikVSXrzE_CkZuZC3k_ukQ%40mail.gmail.com
- **Summary:** A buffer that errored during I/O can have its `BM_IO_ERROR` state
  flag dropped in `TerminateBufferIO`, because of the order in which the buffer
  header is unlocked vs. the flags are updated in the new `UnlockBufHdrExt`
  helper. The race lets a buffer look I/O-clean when its read/write actually
  failed.
- **Skill tag:** `locking` (buffer-header spinlock + flag-update ordering)
- **Ideal answer outline:** Use the `locking` skill's buffer pin/content-lock vs.
  buffer-header-lock distinction and `knowledge/subsystems/storage-buffer.md`
  (the `BufferDesc.state` flag invariants + `TerminateBufferIO` path); confirm
  what ordering keeps `BM_IO_ERROR`/`BM_IO_IN_PROGRESS` consistent. Cite
  `source/src/backend/storage/buffer/bufmgr.c`.
