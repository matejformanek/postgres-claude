### 1. Bug: GiST index-only scans can use tuple descriptor with incorrect alignment when deforming  **[new]**
- message-id: `/message-id/CAH2-WzkGXa2SKnebdW29RT1hCcQBo_p03v3iqif2u9bjzLB-aQ%40mail.gmail.com`
- source: pgsql-hackers ┬Ę Peter Geoghegan ┬Ę Jul 13
- Reports that GiST index-only scans deform the returned `xs_hitup` tuple using
  the executor scan slot's descriptor rather than the index AM's authoritative
  `xs_hitupdesc`; the two can disagree on layout/alignment even when individual
  columns are binary-compatible, producing garbage such as `type with OID nnnnn
  does not exist` on multicolumn GiST index-only scans. Fix is to deform
  `xs_hitup` with `xs_hitupdesc` (already used for `xs_itup`) in
  `nodeIndexonlyscan.c`.
- **skill tag:** `access-method-apis` + `executor-and-planner`
- ideal answer: IOS `xs_hitup` / `xs_hitupdesc` contract via the IndexAmRoutine
  `amgettuple` notes in `access-method-apis`; `IndexOnlyNext` deform path via
  `executor-and-planner` (`nodeIndexonlyscan.c`) ŌĆö the descriptor authority rule
  belongs in `knowledge/subsystems/` under index-only-scan.
