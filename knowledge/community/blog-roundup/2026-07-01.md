## 1. Inside a PostgreSQL Checkpointer Bug: A Production Postmortem

- Author: warda bibi
- URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9nV> (2026-07-01)
- TL;DR:
  - A production postmortem tracing an incident back to the **checkpointer**:
    walks how a checkpoint stall / mis-timed fsync manifested at the application
    layer and how it was diagnosed from `pg_stat_bgwriter` / checkpoint logs

## 2. All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_material and enable_memoize

- Author: Christophe Pettus
- URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9nS> (2026-07-01)
- TL;DR:
  - Next installment of the planner-GUC walkthrough series, this time covering
    `enable_material` (materialize nodes) and `enable_memoize` (the memoize
    caching node for parameterized rescans) — what each does, when the planner
    inserts the node, and what turning it off costs.
  - Practical framing of two executor nodes whose value is entirely about
    rescan economics: materialize spools a subtree, memoize caches inner
    results keyed by parameters across a nested-loop's outer rows.
- **Read if you care about:** the executor's materialize/memoize nodes, rescan
  cost modeling, and the planner GUCs that gate them.
  [[knowledge/subsystems/executor.md]], [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]],
  [[knowledge/idioms/cost-scan-paths.md]]
