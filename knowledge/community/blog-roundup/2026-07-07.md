## 1. Hubert 'depesz' Lubaczewski — Waiting for PostgreSQL 20: Add backend-level lock statistics

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9ow>
- **Published:** 2026-07-06

TL;DR:
- A "Waiting for PostgreSQL 20" writeup of the committed **per-backend lock
  statistics** feature: PG 20 gains the ability to retrieve, for an individual
  backend, how many locks it has waited on and the cumulative wait time —
  attribution that previously required sampling `pg_stat_activity.wait_event`
  externally and inferring totals.
- Walks the new accessor surface with worked examples, showing how per-backend
  wait counts/times let you pin lock contention to a specific session rather than
  reading only cluster- or database-aggregate numbers.

**Read if you care about:** lock observability, the pgstat per-backend
cumulative-stats surface, or diagnosing contention per session. Strong corpus
material — it's the operator-facing view of the same per-backend pgstat push
visible on -hackers this window (see the *per-backend commit/rollback counters*
thread in `knowledge/community/threads/2026-07-07.md`). Ties to
`[[knowledge/skills/pgstat-framework]]` (per-backend stats) and
`[[knowledge/skills/locking]]` (wait-event accounting). `[from-website]`

## 2. Michael Banck — Replication Deadlock Bug in Current Postgres Releases 14–16

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9os>
- **Published:** 2026-07-06

TL;DR:
- Reports and dissects a **MultiXactOffsetSLRU deadlock regression** affecting
  streaming replication and PITR replay on current PG 14–16 point releases: under
  specific multixact-heavy workloads the standby's replay can deadlock against the
  SLRU access path, stalling recovery.
- Explains the mechanism at the SLRU-buffer / multixact-offset level and the
  conditions that trigger it, with guidance on detection and the fix/mitigation
  status across the affected branches.

**Read if you care about:** MultiXact internals, SLRU buffer locking, standby
replay deadlocks, or the reliability of streaming replication / PITR on 14–16.
Directly on-corpus: `[[knowledge/skills/multixact]]` (offset SLRU),
`[[knowledge/skills/slru-infrastructure]]` (buffer locking), and
`[[knowledge/skills/physical-replication]]` (standby replay). The same author's

## 3. Richard Yen — Are You .ready?

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9ox>
- **Published:** 2026-07-06

TL;DR:
- Explains the WAL-archiving status files under `pg_wal/archive_status/` — the
  `.ready` and `.done` marker files that the archiver
  (`[[knowledge/skills/backup-and-recovery]]`, pgarch.c) uses to track which WAL
  segments are eligible for `archive_command` / `archive_library` and which have
  been successfully archived.
- Clarifies the common operational confusion: a pile-up of `.ready` files means
  archiving has fallen behind (or is failing), pinning WAL on disk; the post walks
  the lifecycle from segment completion → `.ready` → archiver picks it up →
  `.done`, and how it relates to (and is distinct from) streaming replication's
  WAL retention.

**Read if you care about:** WAL archiving mechanics, the archiver process, why
`pg_wal` fills up, or `archive_command` troubleshooting. Solid corpus material for
`[[knowledge/skills/backup-and-recovery]]` (WAL archiving, `pgarch.c`,
`archive_status/`). `[from-website]`

## 4. Christophe Pettus — All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_partitionwise_aggregate

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9oz>
- **Published:** 2026-07-07

TL;DR:
- The next installment of the "All Your GUCs in a Row" series covers
  **`enable_partitionwise_aggregate`** — the planner GUC that lets `GROUP BY` /
  aggregation be pushed **below** an Append over a partitioned table, aggregating
  each partition independently before combining, rather than appending all
  partition rows first and aggregating once at the top.
- Frames the tradeoff the GUC exists to gate: partitionwise aggregation can
  dramatically cut work (and enable partitionwise *parallel* aggregation) when the
  grouping key aligns with the partition key, but it **increases planning time and
  memory** (more paths, more hash tables), which is why it defaults off — the same
  memory-for-speed tradeoff the series' `enable_partitionwise_join` entry covered.

**Read if you care about:** partitionwise aggregation, the planner's partitioned
aggregation paths, or the `enable_partitionwise_*` GUC family. Corpus fit:
`[[knowledge/skills/planner-cost-model]]` and `[[knowledge/skills/executor-and-planner]]`
(partition-aware aggregation). Continues the planner-GUC arc after the 07-06
`enable_partition_pruning` and 07-04/07-05 `enable_parallel_append` /
`enable_parallel_hash` posts. `[from-website]`
