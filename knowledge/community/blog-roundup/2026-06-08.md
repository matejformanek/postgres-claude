## 1. All Your GUCs in a Row: `data_sync_retry`

- **Author:** Christophe Pettus · **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **URL:** https://postgr.es/p/9lu
- TL;DR:
  - Walks `data_sync_retry` — a postmaster-context boolean, default `off` — and
    the history behind it: it governs whether PostgreSQL **retries vs. PANICs**
    when an `fsync()` fails. Default `off` means a failed fsync triggers a PANIC
    (crash-restart), because retrying fsync after an error can *silently lose the
    dirty page* on Linux (the "fsyncgate" lesson).
  - The setting exists so operators on platforms with sound fsync-retry
    semantics can opt back into retrying; on Linux you almost always want it
    `off`.
- **Read if you care about:** durability guarantees, the fsyncgate fallout, why
  PG would rather crash than risk a lost write. cf.
  [[knowledge/architecture/wal.md]].

## 2. All Your GUCs in a Row: `data_directory_mode`

- **Author:** Christophe Pettus · **Date:** 2026-06-07
- **URL:** https://postgr.es/p/9ls
- TL;DR:
  - Covers `data_directory_mode` (PG 11+): the permission mask on `PGDATA`,
    allowing `0750` (group read) instead of the historical owner-only `0700`, so
    an **unprivileged backup process** in the right group can read the cluster
    files without being the postgres superuser.
  - A read-only/informational GUC reflecting the mode chosen at `initdb`;
    relevant to backup-tooling and least-privilege operational setups.
- **Read if you care about:** filesystem-level least privilege, backup-process
  access, `initdb`-time directory permissions.
