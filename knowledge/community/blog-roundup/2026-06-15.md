## New this window (published after the 06-14 run)

### 1. Why Postgres Doesn't Have remote_receive — And What Happened When I Tried It
- **Author:** Robins Tharakan · 2026-06-15 12:13 · <https://postgr.es/p/9lS>
- TL;DR:
  - Prototypes a memory-only WAL-receive mode (skip the standby's disk write of
    incoming WAL before apply) to chase a replication-latency win.
  - Finds network round-trip time, not the standby's disk write, dominates —
    so the "savings" mostly evaporate, which is *why* PG never shipped such a knob.
- **Read if you care about:** the WAL receiver path, streaming-replication
  latency budgets, why a plausible optimization doesn't pay off.
  [[knowledge/architecture/replication.md]] · [[knowledge/architecture/wal.md]]

### 2. The NULL in your NOT IN
- **Author:** Radim Marek · 2026-06-14 21:46 · <https://postgr.es/p/9lQ>
- TL;DR:
  - Walks the classic `NOT IN (subquery)` trap: a single NULL in the subquery
    makes the whole predicate return no rows, via SQL three-valued logic.
  - Recommends `NOT EXISTS` / anti-join framing, which the planner can also
    optimize better.
- **Read if you care about:** three-valued logic, anti-join semantics (ties to
  today's hackers thread #2, "Reduce LEFT/FULL JOIN to ANTI JOIN").
  [[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer.md]]
