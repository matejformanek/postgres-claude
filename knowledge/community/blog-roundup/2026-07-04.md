## 1. All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_parallel_append

- Author: Christophe Pettus
- URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9o6> (2026-07-04)
- TL;DR:
  - Next installment of the "All Your GUCs in a Row" series, this time on
    **`enable_parallel_append`** — the planner GUC that governs whether an
    `Append` node (UNION-ALL branches, or a partitioned scan's per-partition
    subplans) may run its children in **parallel** across worker processes,
    rather than a serial parallel-oblivious `Append` that runs children one at a
    time. Walks how a *parallel-aware* `Append` distributes its subplans among
    the available workers so multiple partitions / branches scan concurrently,
    versus the parallel-*oblivious* fallback.
  - Frames when the GUC matters in practice: partition-wise scans over many
    partitions and wide UNION-ALL fan-outs are where parallel append converts
    idle worker slots into concurrent partition scans; it also touches the subtle
    point that a partial (parallel) `Append` and a non-partial `Append` cost and
    behave differently, and that turning the GUC off forces the serial shape.
- Read if you care about: parallel query planning, partition-wise scan
  parallelism, or the `Append`/`Parallel Append` distinction in `EXPLAIN`.
  Pairs directly with this cycle's async-`Append` correctness thread (see
  today's hackers roundup thread 7) — one is the *parallel* Append, the other
  the *asynchronous* Append; the blog is a clean primer on the former.
  `[[knowledge/subsystems/executor.md]]`,
  `[[knowledge/idioms/parallel-gather-merge.md]]`,
  `[[knowledge/subsystems/partitioning.md]]`.
