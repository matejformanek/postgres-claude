## 1. Christophe Pettus — All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_partition_pruning

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9on>
- **Published:** 2026-07-06 01:00 UTC

TL;DR:
- The next installment of the "All Your GUCs in a Row" series covers
  **`enable_partition_pruning`** — the planner GUC governing whether PG eliminates
  partitions that provably cannot contain matching rows. The post distinguishes
  the two pruning phases: **plan-time** pruning (constants known at planning) and
  **execution-time** pruning (parameters known only at execute, e.g. generic
  plans and parameterised nested-loop inner scans).
- Explains the practical consequence — with pruning off, a query against a
  1000-partition table scans (or at least opens) every partition; execution-time
  pruning is what makes generic prepared-statement plans over partitioned tables
  viable — and notes pruning interacts with the partitionwise-join/aggregate GUCs.

**Read if you care about:** partition pruning internals, plan-time vs.
execution-time pruning, generic plans over partitioned tables, or the
`PartitionPruneInfo`/`ExecInitPartitionPruning` machinery. Strong corpus
material — pairs directly with today's hackers thread *"generic plans and
'initial' pruning"* (Amit Langote) and is a natural `[[link]]` from
`[[knowledge/skills/executor-and-planner]]`'s pruning section. Completes the
`Parallel *` + pruning GUC arc alongside the 07-04/07-05 `enable_parallel_append`
/ `enable_parallel_hash` posts. `[from-website]`

## 2. Hans-Juergen Schoenig — Why pg_hardstorage has no incremental chain

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9oo>
- **Published:** 2026-07-06 05:00 UTC

TL;DR:
- A design-rationale post on why a storage/backup layer deliberately forgoes an
  **incremental backup chain** (the full → incremental → incremental → … lineage
  that most backup tools maintain) in favour of always-independent backups. Walks
  through the failure modes of long incremental chains: a single corrupt or
  missing link invalidates every backup after it, restore time grows with chain
  length, and chain bookkeeping becomes its own reliability liability.
- Frames the tradeoff in PG terms — incremental backup (the WAL-summarizer-based
  `pg_basebackup --incremental` machinery in recent PG) buys storage/bandwidth
  savings at the cost of restore-time chain-walking and chain-integrity risk;
  the post argues for the opposite point on that curve for its use case.

**Read if you care about:** backup architecture, the PG incremental-backup / WAL
summarizer design and its chain-dependency tradeoffs, or restore-time reliability
reasoning. Adjacent to `[[knowledge/skills/wal-and-xlog]]` (WAL summarization) and
the physical-backup material. `[from-website]` / [inferred]
