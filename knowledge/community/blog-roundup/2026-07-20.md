## 1. All Your GUCs in a Row: extra_float_digits
- **Author:** Christophe Pettus
- **URL:** https://postgr.es/p/9q1 · 2026-07-20
- Walks through `extra_float_digits`: historically it forced a choice between
  human-readable float output and round-trip-exact precision.
- Since PostgreSQL 12 the shortest-exact float output algorithm means the
  default gives both — readable *and* exact — so the old "bump it to 3 for
  dumps" advice is largely obsolete.
- **Read if you care about:** float/double output fidelity, `pg_dump`
  round-tripping, or the GUC-explainer series.

## 2. Moving to pg_hardstorage without changing your recovery strategy
- **Author:** Hans-Jürgen Schönig
- **URL:** https://postgr.es/p/9q2 · 2026-07-20
- Migration playbook for adopting `pg_hardstorage` alongside an existing
  backup/PITR setup without tearing up the recovery strategy.
- Emphasis on operational continuity — layering the new storage in while
  `restore_command` / base-backup workflows keep working unchanged.
- **Read if you care about:** storage migration, backup/recovery operational
  continuity, staged rollout of a storage layer.
