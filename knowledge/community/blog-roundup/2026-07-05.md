## 1. Radim Marek — VACUUM at the Page Level

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9ol>
- **Published:** 2026-07-05 14:31 UTC

TL;DR:
- A deep, byte-level walkthrough of what VACUUM does to a single heap page:
  it snapshots the page header, the line-pointer (`ItemId`) array, and tuple
  storage before/after, and shows the three-phase arc — scan for dead tuples,
  remove index entries, then reclaim heap line pointers and compact free space —
  as concrete on-page state transitions rather than prose.
- Makes visible the distinction between a dead tuple's *storage* being reclaimed
  vs. its **line pointer** being retired to `LP_UNUSED` (and why the latter can't
  happen until all indexes referencing that TID are cleaned), plus how the page's
  free space and the FSM view of it relate.

**Read if you care about:** heap-AM page layout, VACUUM's three phases,
`ItemId`/line-pointer lifecycle, or the FSM↔heap-page relationship. Strong
corpus material — pairs directly with today's hackers thread #1 (*Missing FSM
Update when Updating VM On-access*) and is a natural `[[link]]` from any
`knowledge/subsystems/access-heap.md` or `storage-freespace.md` page-level
section. `[from-website]`

## 2. Christophe Pettus — All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_parallel_hash

- **URL:** <https://postgr.es/p/9o8>
- **Published:** 2026-07-05 01:00 UTC

TL;DR:
- The next installment of the "All Your GUCs in a Row" series covers
  **`enable_parallel_hash`** — the planner GUC governing whether a parallel hash
  join builds a **single shared hash table** in DSM that all workers populate and
  probe, versus each worker building its own private copy. The shared build lets
  N workers cooperatively construct one table (with a barrier at the build/probe
  transition) instead of paying N× the build memory and work.
- Explains the practical consequence: parallel hash shines when the inner side is
  large enough that a per-worker private hash table would blow `work_mem` N times
  over, and that turning the GUC off forces the parallel-oblivious per-worker
  shape.

**Read if you care about:** parallel query execution, hash-join internals, the
DSM/shared-hash-table + barrier mechanics of `nodeHashjoin`/`nodeHash`, or
`work_mem` accounting under parallelism. Companion to yesterday's
`enable_parallel_append` piece — together they cover the two main
`Parallel *`-vs-serial planner GUCs. Pairs with
`[[knowledge/skills/parallel-query]]`. `[from-website]`
