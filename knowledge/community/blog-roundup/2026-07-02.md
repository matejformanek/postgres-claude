## 1. All Your GUCs in a Row: enable_mergejoin

- Christophe Pettus. URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9nZ> (2026-07-02)
- TL;DR:
  - Next entry in the running planner-GUC series: what `enable_mergejoin=off`
    actually does — it does not forbid merge joins, it adds a large
    `disable_cost` penalty so the planner avoids them unless no alternative
    exists, the same soft-disable mechanism the whole `enable_*` family uses.
  - Frames when a merge join is genuinely the right shape (both inputs already
    sorted on the join key, e.g. from index scans or an upstream sort) versus
    when the planner picks it and shouldn't, and why toggling the GUC is a
    diagnostic probe rather than a production fix.
- **Read if you care about:** the planner's join-method selection and the
  soft-disable semantics of the `enable_*` GUCs.
  `[[knowledge/idioms/cost-join-paths]]`, `[[knowledge/subsystems/optimizer]]`.

## 2. Extreme Rescue: PostgreSQL Full-File Ransomware Recovery at Epic Difficulty

- Zhang Chen. URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9n-> (2026-07-02)
- TL;DR:
  - A recovery war-story: reconstructing a cluster after ransomware encrypted the
    data files, working from the on-disk page structures that survived — walks
    the heap page layout, the control file, and pg_class/pg_attribute bootstrap
    to rebuild enough catalog to salvage user tables.
  - Doubles as an accessible tour of PostgreSQL's on-disk format from a forensic
    angle: page headers, item pointers, tuple layout, and how much of a relation
    can be recovered when the catalog is partially destroyed.
- **Read if you care about:** on-disk page/tuple format, catalog bootstrap, and
  last-resort data forensics. `[[knowledge/data-structures/heap-tuple-layout]]`,
  `[[knowledge/subsystems/access-heap]]`.

## 3. Introducing pg-healthcheck: PostgreSQL Health Diagnostics

- Ahsan Hadi. URL: <https://postgr.es/p/9nX> (2026-07-01 14:18)
- TL;DR:
  - Announces `pg-healthcheck`, a diagnostics tool that samples the standard
    observability surface (pg_stat_activity, pg_stat_database, bloat/vacuum
    stats, replication lag, lock waits) and reports a prioritised health summary.
  - Practically a consumer of exactly the cumulative-statistics framework the
    -hackers pgstat cluster is building out this week (see
    `knowledge/community/threads/2026-07-02.md` threads 1–3) — the tool's value
    scales with what the pgstat views expose.
- **Read if you care about:** the monitoring/observability consumer side of the
  pgstat views and standard health-diagnostic queries.
  `[[knowledge/idioms/pgstat-flush-timing]]`,
  `[[knowledge/subsystems/contrib-pg_stat_statements]]`.
