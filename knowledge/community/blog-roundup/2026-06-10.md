## 1. All Your GUCs in a Row: deadlock_timeout
- **Author:** Christophe Pettus — **URL:** https://postgr.es/p/9lC (Jun 10)
- TL;DR:
  - Next in Pettus's per-GUC series: `deadlock_timeout` is the interval a backend
    waits on a lock **before** running deadlock detection — it is deliberately
    *not* a deadlock "give up" timer but the cost-control knob that keeps the
    O(waiters) `CheckDeadLock()` graph walk off the hot path for the common case
    where the lock is simply held briefly. `[from-website]`
  - The post's practical angle is the usual footgun: setting it too low makes
    every short lock wait pay for a deadlock check, while it also doubles as the
    floor for `log_lock_waits` logging. `[inferred]`
- **Read if you care about:** lock-manager tuning and *why* deadlock detection is
  lazy/deferred rather than continuous. Notably timely — it lands the same day as
  the pgsql-hackers thread reporting the **deadlock detector failing to activate
  on a hot standby** ([[knowledge/community/threads/2026-06-10.md]] §5), the
  exact machinery this GUC gates. cf. [[knowledge/subsystems/storage-lmgr.md]],
  [[knowledge/idioms/locking-overview.md]].
