## 2. PostgreSQL vs Destructive Time Travel: The Year 2038 Problem — Abhisek Goswami

https://postgr.es/p/9q8

- Explains why PostgreSQL is immune to the Unix Y2038 32-bit `time_t`
  overflow: internal timestamps are 64-bit microsecond integers since the
  int64-datetime switch, not 32-bit epoch seconds.
- Contrasts PG's `timestamptz` storage with legacy 32-bit systems.

**Read if you care about:** timestamp storage internals, temporal type
representation. [[knowledge/skills/jsonpath-and-jsonb]] (sibling: datetime adt)
