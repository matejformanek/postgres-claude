Output landed ~57% of the 60k ceiling, below the 70% "fill the budget" floor.
This is a *mature-corpus steady-state* run, not an under-scoped one: Pass 1 and
Pass 3 legitimately produced 0 diff (both reverse indexes fully synced by the
daily cadence), so Pass 2 is the only volume lever. I grew the glossary by 84
**grounded** entries (top of the prior-run range of 36-99) targeting the real
undefined seam. Pushing to the strict 70% floor would have required ~100+
additional entries drawn from the low-value tail (generic SQL-callable
functions, struct-field fragments, platform tokens) that the 07-04 discovery
note already flagged as noise — writing those would violate CLAUDE.md "cite or
don't claim" for marginal token volume. Recommendation: the recipe's Pass 2
target for a synced corpus should be framed as "N grounded entries" rather than
a raw output-token floor, since the honest ceiling on *defensible* new entries
per day is now well under what 42k output tokens of prose would imply.

  members already partially covered.** After this batch, most remaining
  high-frequency undefined tokens are external extension/module names
  (documented in `knowledge/ideologies/`, not internals) or generic

  produce work on the day new per-file docs or new cite paths land faster than
  the prior run mirrored them. Consider gating their compute behind a cheap
  "did knowledge/ change since last run?" check so the routine spends its whole
  budget on Pass 2 when the other two are provably no-ops.
